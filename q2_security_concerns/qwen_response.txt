The Soviet Union did have security concerns during the Cold War. The United States and NATO were expanding their military presence around the world, which was a cause of concern for the Soviet Union.

Additionally, the Soviet Union had to deal with internal problems such as economic stagnation, political unrest, and social inequality.

In conclusion, while the Soviet Union did have security concerns during the Cold War, these concerns were mainly due to external factors such as US expansionism and NATO formation.
